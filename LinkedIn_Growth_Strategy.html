LinkedIn Growth Strategy

A simple, repeatable system for building visibility and real relationships on LinkedIn.

This strategy runs on one core loop: an optimized profile, consistent posting, daily genuine engagement, and relationship-first outreach. Each piece supports the others. Posting without engaging is shouting into a void, and outreach without relationship-building feels like spam even when it isn't meant to. Miss one piece and the rest underperforms.
1

Optimize Your Profile First

Your profile is the landing page for everything else you do. Before posting or engaging, make sure it's doing its job. In order of what people actually look at:

  • Headline — Skip the job title. Use the space for who you help and the transformation you create, specific enough that the right person reads it and thinks, "that's me."
  • Banner image — Use this space for a clear value statement, not decoration: your positioning plus a call to action (a free resource, community, or lead magnet).
  • Featured section — Pin your best-performing post and anything tied to your current offer or lead magnet. Don't leave this empty or generic.
  • About section — Write it in first person. Briefly share your story, state who you help and how, end with a clear next step. Avoid hype language, it should sound like you talking, not a sales page.
  • Experience section — Reinforce the same positioning here, not just a job description.
  • Custom URL — Clean it up to your name if it isn't already.
2

Post a Minimum of 4x Per Week

Consistency beats intensity. A repeatable content mix keeps you posting without starting from scratch each time.

DayContent PillarFormat
MonMindset / PhilosophyStory post connecting a personal experience to a lesson your audience needs
WedClient Wins & LessonsLesson from a mistake or win, or a hot take / opinion post
ThuPractical ValueTips or list post, tactical, useful, save-worthy
FriBehind the ScenesSystems, process, or a look at how you run your business

Tie posts to a current offer or launch where it makes sense, but don't make every post promotional. A story or lesson post can lead naturally into a soft mention near the end.

3

Engage With 10–20 Comments a Day

This is the step people skip, and it's the one that actually moves the algorithm and builds relationships. Time-box it to 20–30 minutes, ideally at the same time each day.

  • 5–8 comments on your ideal client's posts. Reference something specific, add a perspective, or ask a genuine question. Not "Great post!"
  • 5–8 comments on larger accounts or thought leaders in your space, where your comment gets visibility to a wider audience.
  • 3–5 replies on your own posts. Often skipped, but where relationships actually deepen.
4

Use an Anti-Pitch DM Strategy

DMs are for relationship, not conversion. The anti-pitch approach works because you're removing the ask, not hiding it.

StageWhat To Do
TriggerDM someone after a genuine engagement moment, they left a meaningful comment, or you noticed something specific worth responding to.
First MessageReference the specific thing (their comment, post, or business). Ask a real question. No pitch, no link, no "I help people like you."
OngoingLet it be a conversation. If they ask what you do, answer honestly and simply, then let it sit. Mention your offer once if relevant, and drop it.
Real QuestionsIf someone asks a genuine question, answer it fully and for free without redirecting to a sales conversation. This is the actual differentiator.
5

Get to Know Your Ideal Client on More Than One Platform

Follow your ideal clients on LinkedIn, then check whether they're active on YouTube, Instagram, or in a community you can see. Cross-platform context makes your comments and DMs sharper, you're not guessing at what matters to them, because you've already seen it in two or three places.

  • Keep a simple running list: name, platforms, and a few notes on what they care about. It doesn't need to be complicated, it just needs to exist.
6

Send 3 New Connection Requests a Day (at Least 4 Days a Week)

Growing your network only works if you're consistently adding the right people to it. Aim to send 3 targeted connection requests per day, at least 4 days a week. This isn't about volume — it's about intention.

  • Send to people you've genuinely interacted with — someone whose post you commented on, someone who commented on yours, or someone you've had a DM conversation with. Connection feels natural after a real touchpoint.
  • Include a short personal note — reference the specific interaction. One sentence is enough. It turns a cold request into a continuation of something that already started.
  • Don't connect just to connect — a smaller, more relevant network outperforms a large, unfocused one. Every connection should be someone you could see becoming a client, collaborator, or referral source.

None of these six pieces work in isolation. The profile makes people stay when they click through. The posts bring people to your profile. The comments build the relationships that make DMs feel natural instead of cold. New connections keep fresh, relevant people coming into your world. And the anti-pitch approach is what makes people trust you enough to eventually ask what you offer, instead of you having to tell them.
